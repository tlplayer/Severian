## 4. Current Pricing Applies to Continued Use

Commercial license fees are based on the pricing schedule in effect during the period in which Severian is commercially used.

Pricing is not permanently attached to:

* a particular Severian version;
* the date on which Severian was downloaded;
* the date on which commercial use began;
* the price previously charged for that version;
* the continued use of an older release; or
* a user’s decision not to install later releases.

Continued commercial use of any Severian version after a new pricing schedule becomes effective is subject to the new pricing schedule.

For example, if commercial use of Severian version 0.8 costs $1 per Covered Product during 2020 and the applicable fee is changed to $2 beginning in 2026, continued commercial use of version 0.8 during 2026 and afterward is subject to the $2 fee.

The user does not retain the previous price merely by continuing to use version 0.8 or another older version.

A pricing change applies prospectively from its stated effective date. It does not change fees already paid or properly accrued for periods preceding that effective date.

## Revenue-Based Commercial Pricing

### 1. Royalty

For each Covered Product or Covered Service, the commercial licensee shall pay an annual royalty equal to:

[
\text{Royalty} = \text{Applicable Royalty Rate} \times \text{Covered Revenue}
]

Unless another rate is published by the Licensor or agreed to in writing, the Applicable Royalty Rate is **one percent (1%)**.

### 2. Covered Revenue

“Covered Revenue” means all gross amounts received or receivable by the licensee or its Affiliates from a Covered Product or Covered Service during the applicable reporting period.

Covered Revenue includes:

* subscription fees;
* usage-based fees;
* licensing fees;
* hosting fees;
* service fees;
* support and maintenance fees;
* implementation and integration fees;
* transaction fees;
* advertising revenue;
* data-access fees;
* API-access fees;
* marketplace revenue;
* consulting revenue directly associated with the Covered Product or Covered Service;
* bundled revenue allocated to the Covered Product or Covered Service; and
* non-cash consideration valued at fair market value.

Covered Revenue is calculated before deduction of:

* employee compensation;
* contractor expenses;
* hosting or compute costs;
* research and development expenses;
* sales commissions;
* marketing expenses;
* general and administrative expenses;
* depreciation;
* financing expenses;
* affiliate charges;
* income taxes; or
* losses from other products or business operations.

Covered Revenue may exclude only:

* separately stated sales, use, value-added, or similar transaction taxes collected from customers and remitted to a government;
* refunds actually paid to customers;
* chargebacks actually incurred; and
* revenue previously reported and subjected to the royalty.

### 3. Covered Product or Covered Service

A “Covered Product or Covered Service” is any product, service, platform, model, system, API, hosted workload, application, or commercial offering that:

* incorporates Severian;
* distributes Severian;
* executes Severian code;
* is compiled, generated, transformed, or optimized using Severian;
* materially depends on Severian during production operation;
* uses Severian to provide functionality sold to customers; or
* could not provide substantially equivalent functionality without replacing Severian with another material technology.

### 4. Royalty Rates by Use

Unless otherwise published or agreed in writing, the following rates apply:

| Severian use                                                                       |            Royalty rate |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------: |
| Severian used only as a development or compilation tool                            | 0.5% of Covered Revenue |
| Severian materially supports a production product or service                       |   1% of Covered Revenue |
| Severian provides a primary runtime, execution, optimization, or platform function |   2% of Covered Revenue |
| Severian itself is hosted, resold, sublicensed, or offered as a service            |   3% of Covered Revenue |

The Licensor determines the applicable category in good faith based on Severian’s role in the Covered Product or Covered Service.

A separate signed agreement may establish a different rate.

### 5. Bundled Products and Services

When a Covered Product or Covered Service is sold together with other products or services, Covered Revenue shall be determined using:

1. the separately stated price of the Covered Product or Covered Service;
2. the licensee’s ordinary standalone selling price;
3. the relative standalone value of the bundled components; or
4. another reasonable allocation method approved by the Licensor.

The licensee may not avoid or reduce royalties by bundling a Covered Product or Covered Service with unrelated products, services, support, consulting, hardware, or data.

### 6. Indirect Monetization

When a Covered Product or Covered Service does not directly charge users but generates money through advertising, transactions, commissions, lead generation, data sales, sponsorships, or another indirect method, those amounts constitute Covered Revenue to the extent attributable to the Covered Product or Covered Service.

### 7. Affiliate and Related-Party Revenue

Revenue received by an Affiliate, reseller, distributor, marketplace operator, or related party is included in Covered Revenue when the arrangement has the purpose or effect of reducing the reported royalty.

Transactions between related parties must be valued at the greater of:

* the amount actually charged; or
* the fair market value of the applicable product or service.

“Affiliate” means an entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with the licensee.

### 8. Revenue Attribution

When Severian supports only part of a larger service, the licensee may allocate revenue to the Severian-supported portion using a documented and consistently applied method.

The allocation must reasonably reflect the economic value of the Severian-supported functionality.

The licensee may not use an allocation method primarily intended to avoid royalties.

If no reasonable allocation can be established, the entire revenue of the Covered Product or Covered Service is treated as Covered Revenue.

### 9. Reporting and Payment

The licensee shall provide a royalty report and payment within thirty days after the end of each calendar quarter.

Each report must identify:

* each Covered Product or Covered Service;
* gross revenue received or receivable;
* permitted exclusions;
* revenue allocations;
* the applicable royalty rate;
* royalties due; and
* the versions of Severian used.

Reports must be certified as accurate by an authorized representative of the licensee.

### 10. Records and Audit

The licensee shall retain records sufficient to verify royalties for at least five years.

The Licensor may audit those records no more than once per calendar year through an independent accountant bound by confidentiality obligations.

If an audit identifies an underpayment greater than five percent for the audited period, the licensee shall pay:

* the unpaid royalty;
* reasonable interest;
* the reasonable cost of the audit; and
* any other amount permitted by the commercial license.

### 11. Current Pricing

The royalty rate applicable to a reporting period is the rate published for that period, regardless of which Severian version the licensee uses.

Continued use of an older Severian version does not preserve an earlier royalty rate.

For example, if the royalty rate for Severian version 0.8 was 1% in 2020 and the published rate becomes 2% in 2026, commercial use of version 0.8 during 2026 is subject to the 2% rate.

Pricing changes apply prospectively from their stated effective dates and do not recalculate royalties for earlier reporting periods.

A different or fixed royalty rate applies only when stated in a written agreement signed by the Licensor and the licensee.
